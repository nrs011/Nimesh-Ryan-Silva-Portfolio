Computer and Information Security

Introduction to Computer and Information Security

Discuss the importance of systems security and how the loss of security can affect considerably business operations. Use at least one real-world example to support your answer.

System security is the protection of information and property from theft, corruption, and other types of damage, while allowing the information and property to remain accessible and productive. The importance is that it includes the development and implementation of security counter measures. There are a number of different approaches to computer system security, including the use of a firewall, data encryption, passwords and biometrics. The loss of security affecting a business operation can have dramatic and long term effects on a company, such as reputable damage, theft, financial losses, and fines. A real-world example would be the 2011 PlayStation Network outage. The attack occurred between April 17 and April 19, 2011, forcing Sony to turn off the PlayStation Network on April 20. On May 4, Sony confirmed that personally identifiable information from each of the 77 million affected accounts had been exposed. The outage lasted 23 days. At the time, it was one of the largest data security breaches in history. The outage costs were $171 million. Sony stated their belief that Anonymous, a decentralized unorganized loosely affiliated group of hacktivists (hackers and activists) may have performed the attack, but no Anons claimed any involvement. Credit card data was encryption, but Sony admitted that other user information was not encrypted at the time of the intrusion. While the passwords that were stored were not "encrypted", they were transformed using a cryptographic hash function. Sony was fined $395k for security measures so poor they did not comply with the British law. As compensation to users, Sony gave out free PS3 games to help alleviate some of their losses. As a result of the breach, Sony made changes to their terms and conditions.
